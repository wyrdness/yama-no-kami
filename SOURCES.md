## Religious Sensitivity Guidelines

### When Researching Yama-no-kami

> **Critical**: This is **not** folklore study—this is **religious research**

**Appropriate Approach**:
1. **Recognize living tradition**: Active worship, not historical curiosity
2. **Consult religious authorities**: Shinto priests and scholars
3. **Follow shrine etiquette**: Proper behavior at sacred sites
4. **Respect believers**: This is their faith, not folklore
5. **Academic integrity**: Distinguish between religious belief and academic analysis

**Inappropriate Approaches**:
- ❌ Treating as "mythology" or "folklore curiosity"
- ❌ Visiting shrines as tourist spectacle without respect
- ❌ Appropriating religious symbols or practices
- ❌ Trivializing or mocking beliefs
- ❌ Ignoring religious context

## Visiting Mountain Shrines

### Respectful Shrine Visitation

**Basic Etiquette**:
- Bow at torii gate entrance
- Purify at temizuya (water pavilion)
- Make appropriate offerings
- Pray respectfully
- Follow posted guidelines
- Don't photograph restricted areas
- Maintain quiet, respectful demeanor

**Learning More**:
- Consult shrine offices for information
- Attend public ceremonies appropriately
- Read educational materials provided
- Respect "no entry" areas
- Ask permission before special access
